01 — The Concept

Modern biology has redefined the human body as a holobiont — a multispecies entity composed of bacteria, fungi, microbes, and human cells. For centuries, humans have colonised the body as strictly human territory, but the sterile image of the individual has now been overturned.

I, Holobiont? begins from the question of what happens when, instead of ignoring our microbial companions, we live in service of them. What new perceptions arise when we acknowledge our bacterial, fungal and microbial selves not as threats or strangers, but as partners in a delicate ecology of survival?

Through artistic practice we dive into the lived sensation of being more-than-human, exploring what it means to carry vulnerabilities and fears alongside curiosity and wonder. Skin is no longer treated as a boundary between inner and outer, but as a porous interface.

03 — Working Methods

Our working methods intertwine embodied exploration, biological studies, sound recordings, writing and shared practices in different locations and communities. We allow our microbial clouds to evoke skin-inspired movement improvisation — always aware of how our bacteria are constantly shedding, merging, and reshaping one another.

We analyse our gut microbiomes, ferment food, and build homemade bioreactors that cultivate bacterial cultures, learning to sense and balance our microbial selves not only through data but through intuition.

We create soundscapes inspired by quorum sensing — a microbial communication process where chemical signals trigger collective behaviour — translating this phenomenon into sonic worlds that pulse through the performance.

Bus rides, meals, and walks in nature all become laboratories of microbial awareness.

05 — The Artists

Janis Heldmann

Dancer · Choreographer · Sound Designer

A German dancer, performer and musician with roots in both street dance and contemporary performance. Twice German champion and world-level breakdance finalist, later shifting toward contemporary dance with an MA in Acoustics and Audio Technology from Aalto University.

Nella Turkki

Choreographer · Dramaturg · Performer

A Finnish multidisciplinary dance and theatre artist and certified embodiment coach. Previous works include ILMA (Zodiak 2020), Metsän alaiset (2022), and Lavatanssit (Tanztausch Festival Köln 2023). Holds an MA in Dance Arts from Uniarts Helsinki.

Since 2019 Janis and Nella have developed a collaborative practice that merges dance, sound, and scientific engagement — producing participatory and interdisciplinary performances that challenge cultural assumptions and open imaginative spaces of ecological reflection.
